# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

## Prevention Tips

- Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, bank account details, or passwords with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank or authority.  

- Always verify payment requests carefully before approving them on UPI apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. Receiving money does not require entering your UPI PIN.  

- Do not click on unknown or suspicious links received through SMS, email, or social media, as they may lead to fake websites.  

- Avoid installing apps from unknown sources, especially screen-sharing apps, as they can give attackers access to your device.  

- Use strong and unique passwords for all accounts and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.  

- Be cautious while using public Wi-Fi and avoid performing financial transactions on unsecured networks.  

- Do not respond immediately to urgent calls or messages asking for personal or financial information without verification.  

- Regularly monitor your bank account and transaction history to detect any suspicious activity early.  

- Limit sharing personal information on social media to reduce the risk of targeted attacks.  

---

## Financial / UPI Safety    

- Never approve unknown or unexpected UPI collect requests, even if they appear genuine.  

- Always check the receiver’s name and UPI ID before sending money. Even small mistakes can lead to loss of funds.  

- Do not scan QR codes sent by unknown people, especially if they claim you will receive money after scanning.

# Reflection

While researching identity theft and cybercrime, what surprised me the most was that many scams do not involve advanced hacking but rely on manipulating people through fear and urgency. Cybercriminals take advantage of human behavior rather than technical weaknesses.

As a result, one habit I will change is being more cautious while handling financial transactions and online requests. I will always verify details before taking action and avoid responding to suspicious messages or calls. This task has made me more aware of digital risks and encouraged me to follow safer online practices in my daily life.
